About

I am a dynamic professional with a completed Master's degree in Finance and Financial Management from the European School of Management and Business, currently pursuing a second Master's in Applied Economics at Universidad de los Andes. My dual academic background in Industrial Engineering and Economics from Pontificia Universidad Javeriana enables me to approach financial and strategic challenges with a structured, analytical, and results-driven mindset.

My career has focused on strategic and financial consulting for the banking, investment banking, and asset management sectors. At Management Solutions, I advise leading financial institutions on capital markets strategy, derivatives valuation, regulatory risk measurement, and operating model transformation, working directly with senior executives and C-level stakeholders. I combine strong financial knowledge with advanced skills in data analytics and quantitative modeling to generate actionable insights that support high-impact decisions.

I also lead the Sekia Research Group at Pontificia Universidad Javeriana, coordinating applied research across econometrics, education policy, and social outcomes in Colombia.

With a solid track record in applied research, project management, and leading multidisciplinary teams, I excel at solving complex problems through innovative thinking and strategic execution. I am eager to contribute my strategic vision, quantitative expertise, and leadership skills to top-tier consulting firms, asset management companies, or global financial institutions.

Inflation Forecasting in Colombia: A Time Series Approach

Working Paper

2025 – Present · Universidad de los Andes

Benchmarking inflation forecasting models for Colombia — Random Walk, AR, ARX, BVAR with Minnesota prior, and TVP-BVAR-SV (Primiceri 2005) — with emphasis on density forecast calibration and monetary transmission time-variation. Targeting Q2 2026 submission.

Effects of Coca Crops on Academic Performance in Colombia (2018–2021)

Research Paper

November 2023 · Pontificia Universidad Javeriana / Sekia Research Group

Municipal-level analysis using SABER 11 test scores to quantify the effect of coca cultivation on educational outcomes. Finds that a one standard deviation increase in cultivated hectares reduces average scores by approximately 0.03 standard deviations. Winner of the FENADECO Research Seedbeds Call and Javeriana's Economics Research Competition.
