About

I am a strategy-oriented professional working in financial services consulting, with a strong analytical foundation spanning industrial engineering, economics, and finance. My work focuses on advising senior stakeholders at financial institutions on capital markets strategy, risk analytics, and operating model transformation.

I hold a B.S. in Industrial Engineering from Pontificia Universidad Javeriana and an M.Sc. in Finance from Escuela Europea de Negocios. I am currently pursuing an M.Sc. in Applied Economics at Universidad de los Andes, where my research interests center on macroeconomics, inflation dynamics, time series econometrics, and monetary policy.

Before my current role at Management Solutions, I worked at JP Morgan Chase, where I built dashboards, data pipelines, and automated workflows for internal operations. I also lead the Sekia Research Group at Pontificia Universidad Javeriana, coordinating applied research projects across data analysis, writing, and academic presentations.

I am broadly interested in the application of quantitative methods to financial markets, policy analysis, and economic research, and I aspire to pursue doctoral studies in economics or finance.
